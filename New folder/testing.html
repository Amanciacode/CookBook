Adobo

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds pork or chicken, cut into serving pieces
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup vinegar
  • 5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon peppercorns
  • 1/2 cup water
  • Cooking oil

    • Instructions

    1. Combine pork or chicken, soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, and peppercorns in a bowl. Marinate for at least 30 minutes or overnight.
    2. Heat oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Brown the marinated meat on all sides.
    3. Pour in the remaining marinade and water. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low and simmer for 45 minutes to 1 hour, or until the meat is tender and the sauce has thickened.
    4. Serve hot with rice.

    Kare-Kare

    Ingredients

    • 2 pounds oxtail, cut into 2-inch pieces
    • 1/2 cup peanut butter
    • 1/4 cup annatto seeds, soaked in 1/2 cup water
    • 1 onion, chopped
    • 3 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1 eggplant, cut into cubes
    • 1 bunch string beans, cut into 2-inch lengths
    • 1 banana blossom, sliced and blanched
    • Shrimp paste (bagoong) to taste
    • Salt and pepper to taste

    Instructions

    1. In a large pot, boil oxtail in water until tender. Skim off any scum that rises to the surface.
    2. Strain the annatto seeds and reserve the water.
    3. In a pan, heat some oil and saute garlic and onions until softened.
    4. Add the peanut butter and cook for a few minutes, stirring constantly, until it becomes fragrant.
    5. Pour in the annatto water and bring to a boil. Reduce heat to low and simmer for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens.
    6. Add the oxtail, eggplant, string beans, and banana blossom to the sauce. Simmer until the vegetables are tender.
    7. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
    8. Serve hot with rice and shrimp paste on the side.

    lumpia

    Ingredients

    • 1 package lumpia wrappers
    • 1/2 pound ground pork or beef
    • 1/2 cup carrots, finely chopped
    • 1/4 cup onion, finely chopped
    • 2 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
    • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
    • 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper
    • Cooking oil for frying

    Instructions

    1. In a pan, heat some oil and saute garlic and onions until softened.
    2. Add ground pork or beef and cook until browned. Drain excess fat.
    3. Add carrots, soy sauce, oyster sauce, and pepper. Cook for a few more minutes until the vegetables are tender.
    4. Let the filling cool slightly.
    5. Place a lumpia wrapper on a flat surface with one corner pointing towards you. Spoon about 2 tablespoons of filling near the bottom corner.
    6. Fold the bottom corner over the filling, then fold in the sides. Roll the lumpia tightly away from you.
    7. Moisten the top corner with water to seal.
    8. Heat oil in a deep fryer or pot to 350°F (175°C).
    9. Fry the lumpia in batches until golden brown and crispy.
    10. Drain on paper towels and serve hot with dipping sauce.

    Lechon

    Ingredients

    • One whole pig, cleaned and dehaired
    • Lemongrass stalks, bruised
    • Garlic cloves, crushed
    • Onions, quartered
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    • Banana leaves or aluminum foil

    Instructions

    1. Stuff the pig's cavity with lemongrass, garlic, and onions.
    2. Season the pig generously with salt and pepper, both inside and out.
    3. Skewer the pig on a long bamboo pole or metal rod.
    4. Roast the pig over charcoal or in a rotisserie oven, turning occasionally, until the skin is crispy and the meat is cooked through. This may take several hours depending on the size of the pig.
    5. Rest the lechon for a few minutes before chopping and serving.
